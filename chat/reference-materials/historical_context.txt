HISTORICAL CONTEXT

ERA OVERVIEW

Gilded Age (c. 1870–1900)
	•	Period of rapid industrialization and economic growth
	•	Large corporations and trusts dominated major industries
	•	Significant wealth inequality and weak government regulation
	•	Common business practices included consolidation, price manipulation, and political influence

Progressive Era (c. 1890–1920)
	•	Period of reform responding to problems of industrialization
	•	Increased public concern about monopolies, corruption, and social inequality
	•	Growth of investigative journalism (“muckraking”)
	•	Reformers sought regulation of business and social improvements

STANDARD OIL AND JOHN D. ROCKEFELLER
	•	John D. Rockefeller was a leading industrialist of the late 19th century
	•	Founder of the Standard Oil Company
	•	Standard Oil became the dominant oil refining business in the United States

Business practices associated with Standard Oil:
	•	Horizontal integration (buying out competitors)
	•	Preferential railroad rates and rebates
	•	Secret agreements with railroads and business partners

South Improvement Company (1872):
	•	A scheme involving railroads and oil refiners
	•	Gave Standard Oil lower shipping rates
	•	Charged competing refiners higher rates
	•	Enabled Standard Oil to pressure or absorb competitors

Timing note:
	•	Many practices described by Ida Tarbell occurred in the 1870s–1880s
	•	Tarbell’s work was published later, during the Progressive Era

⸻

IDA TARBELL
	•	Investigative journalist and leading muckraker
	•	Her father operated an independent oil business that was destroyed by Standard Oil
    •   This personal connection informed her long-term interest in monopoly power and her anti-Rockefeller stance
	•	Author of The History of the Standard Oil Company
	•	Published serially from 1902 to 1904
	•	Conducted extensive research using documents and interviews
	•	Known for critical coverage of monopoly power and corporate behavior
	•	Intended audience included educated readers concerned with business ethics and reform

⸻

JACOB RIIS AND URBAN NEW YORK
	•	Journalist, photographer, and social reformer
	•	Immigrant from Denmark
	•	Experienced poverty and homelessness in New York City
	•	Focused on living conditions of the urban poor

How the Other Half Lives (1890):
	•	Exposed overcrowded tenements, poor sanitation, and child poverty
	•	Used photography alongside written reporting
	•	Aimed to bring public attention to urban living conditions
	•	Influenced public opinion and reform efforts

⸻

NEW YORK CITY IN THE LATE 19TH CENTURY
	•	Rapid population growth due to immigration
	•	Dense tenement housing, especially on the Lower East Side
	•	Poor sanitation and limited regulation
	•	Stark contrast between wealth and poverty

⸻

MUCKRAKING JOURNALISM
	•	Investigative reporting focused on social and economic problems
	•	Sought to expose abuses of power
	•	Often aimed to prompt public awareness and reform
	•	Common during the Progressive Era
